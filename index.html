Monasterio de Tarlac

The Monasterio de Tarlac is a Catholic monastery on top of Mount Resurrection, part of the Zambales Mountain Range on the island of Luzon in the Philippines. It is part of the Mount Resurrection Eco Park in Barangay Lubigan, San José, Tarlac. The monastery houses a relic believed to be a fragment of the True Cross of Jesus.

Place to Eat

Nestled atop the serene hills of Tarlac, Monasterio Mountain View by CAFE TRIBU offers a breathtaking panorama that is perfect for a relaxing getaway.

This charming café provides visitors with a stunning view of the lush landscapes surrounding the Monasterio de Tarlac, making it an ideal spot to unwind and soak in the natural beauty. Enjoy a delightful meal or a refreshing drink while gazing at the majestic 30-foot Christ the Redeemer statue and the well-kept gardens that adorn the area1. Whether you're looking for a peaceful retreat or a picturesque backdrop for your travel photos, CAFE TRIBU promises an unforgettable experience.

Famous Attractions

The 30-foot Statue of the Risen Christ: This impressive statue is a major highlight and a popular photo spot

Canding Falls

Canding Falls is located in Barangay Maasin, San Clemente, Tarlac, and is one of the top eco-tourism and tourist destinations in Tarlac due to its accessibility.

Canding Falls is a pretty easy hike from the Canding River jump-off location, which is 1km away. Canding Falls is a wonderful area to relax in the cool water, take in the scenery, and, most importantly, appreciate the beauty of the entire Canding Falls.

Mount Pinatubo

Mount Pinatubo is located on the shared border of Tarlac, Zambales, and Pampanga.

The active volcano is notorious for its eruption on June 15, 1991, which is considered the second-largest terrestrial eruption of the 20th century.

Place to Eat

Bale Capampangan: A popular local restaurant near Mount Pinatubo, known for its traditional Filipino dishes. It's a great place to experience local flavors

Famous Attractions

Mount Pinatubo is now a tourist attraction in Tarlac and is one of the Philippine volcanoes worth seeing. You can explore the volcano and its surrounding landscapes via Mount Pinatubo tours, which include crater hikes and 4x4 rides.

La Sagrada Familia Parish

La Sagrada Familia Parish in Gerona, Tarlac, is a remarkable religious site known for its intricate design and historical significance.

Built in 2017, the church features a beautifully crafted altar that gained prominence when Pope Francis kissed it during his visit to the Philippines in 2015. Designed by the late Rev. Fr. Alex Bautista, the church reflects a blend of faith, culture, and environment, making it a significant landmark in Tarlac

Place to Eat

Isdaan Floating Restaurant offers a unique dining experience where you can savor delicious Filipino cuisine while seated in floating huts.

It's an ideal spot for families, featuring various activities and entertainment options that make the visit enjoyable for all ages.

Whether you're looking to relax and enjoy the serene ambiance or engage in fun activities, Isdaan Floating Restaurant has something for everyone.

Famous Attractions

Architectural Beauty: The church is admired for its stunning architectural design, both inside and out. The intricate details and the serene ambiance make it a must-visit for those interested in religious architecture

Aquino Center and Museum

The Aquino Center and Museum is established as a tribute to the two icons of democracy. The late Sen. Benigno “Ninoy” Aquino Jr. and his wife, the late President Corazon “Cory” Aquino.

Place to Eat

The Gentle Sparrow Café is a charming spot nestled within the Luisita Golf and Country Club in Tarlac.

This cozy café offers a serene escape with its picturesque setting, featuring a terrace shaded by a massive tent and a bar tucked under the building's old arches.

The menu is straightforward yet delightful, with options ranging from all-day brunch sets to refreshing juices and shakes. It's a perfect stop for a relaxing meal while enjoying the scenic views of the golf course

Famous Attractions

Historical Exhibits: The museum houses a collection of memorabilia, including personal items, photographs, and documents that chronicle the lives of Ninoy and Cory Aquino. Key exhibits include the clothes Ninoy wore during his assassination, which are displayed in a glass case

Goshen Resort and Hotel

Goshen Resort And Hotel resort's structures will truly make you feel that you experiencing an Italian holiday. A unique retreat that transports visitors to ancient Rome with its impressive Roman-inspired architecture.

Place to Eat

Cafe Roma is a delightful café located within the picturesque Goshen Resort and Hotel in Bamban, Tarlac.

This lovely spot offers a cozy ambiance with its Roman-inspired decor, perfectly complementing the resort's overall theme.

Guests can enjoy a variety of menu items, including Italian dishes that enhance the Roman vibe of the place

Famous Attractions

Guests can enjoy various amenities, including swimming pools with Roman-themed backdrops, spacious grounds for events, and a serene environment perfect for family gatherings or romantic getaways.

Located just a few hours' drive from Manila, Goshen Resort offers a distinctive escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Anzap Twin Falls

Anzap Twin Falls is a stunning natural attraction located in Mayantoc, Tarlac.

This 200-foot high waterfall is part of the ASEAN-New Zealand Afforestation Project and is one of the tallest waterfalls in the province1. The falls are composed of two streams originating from the Matibya and Piccan Rivers, making it one of the few twin falls in the Philippines

Place to Eat

Intan Cafe Bistro in Mayantoc, Tarlac, is a charming and intimate spot known for its scenic views of the mountain ranges of Tarlac.

The cafe offers a relaxed atmosphere, making it a perfect place to unwind and enjoy a meal with friends or family. Their menu features a variety of dishes that cater to different tastes, ensuring there's something for everyone

Famous Attractions

ANZAP Twin Falls in Tarlac is a stunning natural attraction where the Matibya and Piccan Rivers converge, creating two majestic waterfalls that stand around 200 feet tall. Established in the 1970s, this area has become a beloved destination for hikers and nature enthusiasts. The rich forestry surrounding the falls offers a lush, green backdrop, making it one of Tarlac's premier hiking spots.

Sitio Tala, “Little Baguio”

Sitio Tala, often referred to as the “Little Baguio” of Tarlac, is a picturesque destination located in San Jose, Tarlac. This wonderful spot is known for its cool climate, scenic mountain views, and lush pine trees, reminiscent of Baguio City.

Place to Eat

Cafétana PH in San Jose, Tarlac, is a delightful mountain-side cafe known for its breathtaking views and serene ambiance.

Nestled in Sitio Baag, this spot offers visitors a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Guests can enjoy a variety of coffee and refreshments while taking in the stunning scenery of the surrounding mountains

Famous Attractions

Visitors to Sitio Tala can enjoy a variety of activities such as hiking, picnicking, and photography. The area is particularly popular among adventure enthusiasts and motorbike riders who appreciate the winding roads and breathtaking landscapes. It's an ideal spot for those looking to unwind and experience nature's tranquility.
